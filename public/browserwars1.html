Browser Wars

One of the first web browsers - Mosaic

What is the browser wars? It all started in the mid-90's. This was a time when people first came online. The internet was pretty new and not as clear as it is today. If it was 1995, you would most likely be using one browser to check out the world wide web - Netscape Navigator. Some students from the University of Illinois were fascinated about the WWW in 1993. One of these students, Mark Andreessen had an idea visualizing a future where everyone can use the internet as part of their daily lives. He worked with his friends (other programmers) to add images, pictures, audio and video capabilities and called it Mosaic.

They posted the user friendly software online and made it a free download which spread drastically.The dream of Bill Gates in 1993 was close to become a reality. About 90% of the world's PC's were running Microsoft.

In order for Mosaic go global it required money. Jim Clark assisted with funds and they worked on how to turn mosaic into a business venture. They recruited a team to help which included Rob McCool and Alec Totic. Later on Mosaic was commercialized by Netscape which enabled access to the WWW. This made it much easier for everyone to get access to content. After Mosaic hit the web it grew more than 300,000%.

The earliest widely used browser -Netscape

Netscape started not long after software engineer Marc Andreessen graduated from the University of Illinois in 1994. Jim Clark and team launched the fastest growing software company the world has ever seen: Netscape communications. Now they would build a new browser based on this. Netscape’s new web browser, Navigator was released in October 1994.

Netscape & Mozilla

At the beginning of Netscape Navigator's decline, Netscape open-sourced the code, and later endowed it to the newly formed non-profit Mozilla. The continued the build for several years recreating another browser which was named Phoenix originally, then the name changed to Firebird then to Firefox.

This browser was the center of the their development efforts which led to Mozilla Firefox 1.0 being released in November 2004. It acquired an growth in shares of the browser market until a peak in 2010.
