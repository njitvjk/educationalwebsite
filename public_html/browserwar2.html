Browser Wars

Posted on October 29, 2021 by Now-U-Know. Images from CreativeCommons
Netscape

In 1995, Netscape hired Gary Reback who was a famous anti-trust lawyer as they knew Bill Gates will be coming after the internet. Netscape and Microsoft had a meeting at Netscape’s corporate headquarters for 6 hours. Microsoft offered $1M to buy their whole business, take it or leave it. Microsoft clearly broke the law. Netscape filed a lawsuit. Jim Clark wanted to take it public as Netscape was the fastest growing company ever.

Internet Explorer

Bill Gates had his entire company hard at work trying to come up with a solution to surpass Netscape. A new web browser of its own was introduced in December 1995: Internet Explorer. Microsoft spread the word if Netscape was used Microsoft will be uninstalled. Microsoft started giving Internet Explorer for free bundled and seamlessly integrated into the Windows OS.

The browser war between Netscape and Microsoft was over in September 1997. Netscape market shares were in single digit and reducing by 1998 and soon after that AOL came and took over.

Microsoft Lawsuit

In the same year, the U.S. Department of Justice launched an anti-trust lawsuit against Bill Gates’ company, Microsoft. The government accused Microsoft of using its windows monopoly to partake in a wide variety of predatory and exclusionary business practices to hinder consumers from accessing Netscape’s products. Gates was shown as not being honest.

The federal judge announced his verdict guilty and recommended the company be broken up causing its market value to drop to $30M overnight. Eventually a federal appeals court agreed with the judgement Microsoft had indeed broken the law but decided the decision was too severe to split the company in two. Gates handed over his position to 2 Steven Ballmer.

Browser Wars Continued On

The browser wars carried on even with the decline of Internet Explorer's market share and the acceptance of other browsers including Firefox, Google Chrome, Safari, Microsoft Edge, and Opera to name a few. Regardless of being the latest entrant into the browser field, Chrome rapidly acquired a superior share of users. With the inception of HTML5 and CSS 3, an advanced age of browser war initiated.This included a global use of smartphones and other mobile devices for searching the WWW. It is a never ending revolution. In the near future there may be a new enhanced browser released to the public use.
